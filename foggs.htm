Fogg's Hotel is a modern first class hotel five minutes from the city. It has 146 rooms, six conference rooms, eight small meeting rooms, a Relax Club, two restaurants that can seat 300 guests and free parking. The city is five minutes away. The hotel changed its name to Foggs Hotel on the 2nd of October in 1992. The name is in honour of Phileas Fogg, who featured in Jules Verne's novel 'Around the World in 80 Days'. Fogg began his journey on the 2nd of October 1872, a 120 years earlier. The theme for the hotel has also been built around this era, resonating the 'Old Factory' period. The book has also been placed in every room. The hotel was built in 1986, affording Fogg's Hotel to takes pride in featuring its modern safety features. The hotel is equipped with emergency exits, heat and smoke detectors. There are built in automatic alarms to the Fire Department. Staff is also well trained in both uses of fire extinguishers and first aid. The hotel also has 24-hr desk service with security patrols and assault alarms. The main entrance is locked at nights and only opened after the clerk has seen the arriving guest.
