Training on the Hauraki Gulf, Auckland, New Zealand

OneWorld was the first of the 2002 challengers to sail in Auckland. Training in New Zealand is the best way to neutralize the advantages that the local defender has over a foreign challenger. Getting an IACC yacht up to its optimum speed requires months of testing and trailing in all conditions. Doing that testing in the Hauraki Gulf allows the afterguard of the boat to study the racing venue in nearly all states of wind and tide. Anyone who watched the 2000 finals between New Zealand and Italy will understand how important that is. By the time the first gun sounds for the Louis Vuitton Cup preliminaries, Peter Gilmour and crew will have sailed approximately for two full years on the Gulf.
