Seattle Yacht Club

In 1992, the Seattle Yacht Club celebrated its 100th year of existence.  On Sept. 3, 1892, officers were chosen to lead the new Seattle Yacht Club.  Membership was $5 each for the first 200 members and $1 per month dues.

The clubhouse, which was built in 1892, was located at the base of Duwamish Head and was moved to its present location on Portage Bay in 1920.  Since that time, the club has grown to approximately 3,000 members and has no "social" membership category.

In September 2000, Commodore Thurman formally issued a challenge to match race in sailboats for the world-renowned America's Cup.

For more information log onto the Seattle Yacht Club website.
